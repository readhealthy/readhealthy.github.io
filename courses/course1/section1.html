Goal: Predict how healthy behaviors can affect health status.

Introduction:

As you might be aware, our choices can affect us in the present. We have all experienced the droopy eyes and fatigue of a short night of sleep. However, these behaviors can have long-standing effects that can follow us deep into our lives. Consider that new research has shown that sleeping too late during adolescence has a strong influence on brain development that is permanent after age 28. Therefore, the benefits of healthy behaviors cannot be stressed enough. By eating nutritious foods, staying physically active, avoiding harmful substances, and ensuring at least 8 hours of sleep per night, we can establish the fundamentals for a life of good health.

Drawing Relationships:

Biology classes can inform us on why certain behaviors are beneficial and/or harmful to our bodies in the long term. For example, tobacco is a harmful substance that contains numerous carcinogens. Carcinogens cause damage to DNA and increase the chances of cancer over time. Acrolein is a carcinogen in tobacco that can physically bind to DNA strands, causing damage, as well as prevent the repair of damaged DNA.

Think & Share: Explain why DNA damage may have long term effects on the body.

English class can help us predict the potential dangers of certain activities. Many materials can be extremely useful to us, but also potentially dangerous. For example, glue is useful to stick things together. However, at high temperatures, glue can decompose into toxic chemicals that damage our bodies. How can we be aware of these hazards? By examining the Safety Data Sheets (SDS). These are free materials that can be used to determine what circumstances different materials are dangerous in. By reading this material we can ensure that we avoid harmful situations.
