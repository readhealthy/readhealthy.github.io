Goal: Analyze the relationship between access to health care and health status.

Introduction:

The ability to find and use health services is a vital skill that contributes to our health. Access to health care has three distinct categories: coverage, services, and timeliness. Coverage is the ability to pay for health care. Health insurance is the primary form of coverage. Next, services are the kinds of health care available to each person. These kinds of services include our doctors, emergency services, and other resources that help promote healthy behaviors. Lastly, timeliness is how fast we can access hospitals and other services when we know we need care. These three categories all affect our health differently.

For example, without coverage, we may have difficulty arranging and paying for treatment. Having adequate services allows for us to access more resources that can benefit our health, such as exercise, diets, and preventive screenings (tests that identify illnesses before we experience any symptoms). Lastly, shorter waiting periods allow us to receive care when we need it.
