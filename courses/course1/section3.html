Goal: Analyze how the environment affects personal health.

Introduction:

There are many outside factors that can influence our health. Our surroundings impact how we feel mentally, physically, socially, and intellectually. Some examples of this include air quality, water quality, and exposure to toxic materials. For example, poor air quality is connected to many diseases including cancer and lung damages. However, keeping the environment clean is a group effort. Everyone must work together to keep the Earth clean.

Drawing Relationships:

Science class helps explain why certain materials could be dangerous to us. When the Earth is exposed to manmade chemicals, they can interact with nature and cause changes to our ecosystem. Because many food resources come from nature, this can cause what we eat and what we drink to harm us.

Think & Share: What kind of chemicals are found in our drinking water?

History helps explain how the environment can have a huge impact on millions of people. By studying history, we can find links between pollution and our own health. Through these connections, we can avoid ruining our surroundings with harmful materials.
