Goal: Analyze how genetics and family history can impact personal health.

Introduction:

Our genes are the instruction manuals for our bodies, providing a guide for our body development. These genes are passed down from our parents to us. In fact, our genes have been passed down through the many generations of our families. Therefore, by looking at diseases that our family members have had, we can also find out what diseases we may be at risk of. This link between family history, genetics, and disease is important for us to catch diseases in early stages, improving our own health.

Drawing Relationships:

In biology, you may have learned about the concept of DNA. Each gene is actually a sequence of DNA that codes for various proteins in our bodies. In humans, DNA is passed on from parents to children in a process known as meiosis. This process combines DNA from both parents, and therefore demonstrates why we should be aware of the family history of disease on both sides of our families.

Sociology and biology together help demonstrate how important our surroundings are. One example of this is genetic epidemiology, and important part of public health. Genetic epidemiologists study the genes of large groups of people and attempt to find a relationship between genetics, disease, and the environment. This information is valuable, and can inform people on what behaviors to avoid based on their genes/genetic susceptibility.
